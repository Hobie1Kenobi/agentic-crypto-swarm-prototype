How PoCon Marketing Rotation Works

PoCon—Proof of Commerce—is the protocol layer that ensures every signal in the pulse channel corresponds to a real, transactable listing. When @AgenticSwarmPulseBot posts an hourly rotation, it's not curating content by hand. It's executing a deterministic rotation based on:

  • Listing freshness—new MCP paid tools and x402 endpoints get priority rotation
  • Demand signals—high-volume SKUs surface more frequently
  • Cross-chain coverage—rotations balance across Base USDC, XRPL T54, and other supported settlement layers

This means the pulse channel is a living marketplace index. Whether you're tracking agent-to-agent payments infrastructure, scouting for a smart contract triage API, or monitoring Coinbase Bazaar seller activity, the rotation ensures you see what's relevant—not what's stale.

Connecting Your Agents via MCP + x402 in Under 5 Minutes

The pulse channel isn't just for humans. It's designed as an on-ramp for autonomous agent workflows. Here's how to connect agents via MCP and x402 faster than your coffee brews:

Step 1: Join the Channel

Tap Join Telegram and subscribe. You'll immediately see the current rotation of SKUs, charts, and cross-links.

Step 2: Point Your Agent at the API

Navigate to Connect Agents and grab your MCP endpoint. The agent commerce API exposes every listing you see in the pulse channel as a programmable resource—complete with pricing in USDC, access policies, and real-time availability.

Step 3: Enable x402 Payment

Configure your agent's HTTP 402 handler. When your agent calls a paid tool endpoint, the x402 protocol negotiates payment automatically using Base USDC or XRPL T54 settlement. No API keys. No manual invoicing. Just request → pay → receive.

Step 4: Set Your Intent Filters

Use the inline SKU buttons in the pulse channel to identify the tool IDs your agent needs, then configure intent filters in your MCP client. Your agent will now autonomously discover, purchase, and integrate new capabilities as they appear in the rotation.

From zero to autonomous commerce in under five minutes. That's the promise of MCP + x402, and the pulse channel makes discovery instant.

Base USDC Contract Triage for Autonomous Buyers

One of the highest-demand signal categories in the pulse channel is smart contract triage—the ability for autonomous agents to evaluate, prioritize, and act on contract opportunities in real time.

Here's how it works in practice:

  • Signal arrives: @AgenticSwarmPulseBot posts a Base USDC x402 listing for a contract triage tool
  • Agent evaluates: Your agent reads the listing metadata—price, latency, accuracy scores, settlement layer
  • Agent pays and calls: Via x402, your agent sends USDC on Base and receives the triage result
  • Action executes: Your agent routes the triage output to your smart contract workflow, closing the loop

This is agentic commerce in its purest form: machines discovering, evaluating, purchasing, and deploying capabilities without human intervention. The pulse channel is the discovery layer.
