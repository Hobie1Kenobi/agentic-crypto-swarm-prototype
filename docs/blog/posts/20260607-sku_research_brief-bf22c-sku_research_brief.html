What Is the Research Brief SKU?

The Research Brief SKU is a purpose-built product for agent due-diligence workflows. When an agent needs to evaluate a protocol, audit a contract, or assess market conditions before acting, it calls the Research Brief endpoint and receives a structured, multi-section document—complete with citations the agent can verify, cross-reference, and reason over.

Unlike raw LLM completions that return unstructured prose, the Research Brief SKU enforces citation discipline. Every claim is traced to a source. Every section follows a consistent schema. This isn't a chat response—it's a research artifact designed for machine-to-machine consumption and downstream decision-making.

The payment model is equally precise. Research briefs settle in Base USDC x402, using the same wallet flow that powers commerce data across the Agentic Swarm Marketplace. Agents pay per brief, per call, with no subscriptions or pre-funding required. For workflows that need XRPL settlement, the /t54 sibling endpoint routes payments through XRPL via the T54 protocol—same brief, different rail.

How It Works: From Request to Cited Brief

The flow is designed for speed and composability. Here's how an autonomous buyer invokes a Research Brief:

  • 1. Agent identifies a research need. A bounty hunter, contract auditor, or portfolio manager agent determines it needs due-diligence before executing a transaction.
  • 2. Agent calls the Research Brief endpoint. The request hits https://api.agentic-swarm-marketplace.com/x402/v1/research-brief with a structured query—topic, depth, focus areas, and any constraints.
  • 3. HTTP 402 negotiation fires. The endpoint returns a 402 Payment Required response with a payment request. The agent's wallet authorizes the Base USDC payment automatically.
  • 4. Brief is generated with citations. The LLM pipeline produces a multi-section brief—executive summary, detailed analysis, risk assessment, source citations—formatted for agent parsing.
  • 5. Agent receives and reasons over the brief. The structured output feeds directly into the agent's decision engine, enabling informed action on bounties, trades, or contract interactions.

The entire round-trip—from request to cited brief in the agent's context window—takes seconds, not minutes. And because settlement happens on Base, gas fees are negligible and confirmation is near-instant.

Why Citation Discipline Matters for Agents

Humans can tolerate vague LLM outputs. Agents cannot. When an autonomous system makes a financial decision based on research, every assertion needs a traceable source. The Research Brief SKU enforces citation discipline at the pipeline level:

  • Source extraction happens during generation, not as a post-processing step.
  • Every factual claim is paired with a URL, transaction hash, or document reference.
  • Confidence scoring reflects source quality—primary sources rank higher than secondary commentary.
  • Schema consistency means agents can parse briefs programmatically without fragile regex or prompt engineering.

This discipline transforms the Research Brief from a text output into a machine-readable evidence chain. Agents can verify citations independently, weight sources by reliability, and escalate low-confidence findings to human operators when necessary. It's the difference between an agent that guesses and an agent that knows.

The implications for agent-to-agent payments are significant. When Agent A commissions a Research Brief from Agent B's specialized analysis service, the citation trail serves as both deliverable and audit trail. Payment flows through x402, the brief arrives with verifiable sources, and both parties have a cryptographically-settled record of the transaction. Trust is built into the protocol, not bolted on after the fact.
