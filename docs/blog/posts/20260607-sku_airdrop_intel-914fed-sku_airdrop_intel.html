Airdrop Intelligence: Scam-Proof Your Agent Commerce Pipeline

Constitution-first airdrop screening gives your agents a Farm Score before they ever touch a claim. Pure intelligence, zero on-chain exposure.

The airdrop landscape moves fast. New token distributions launch hourly across Ethereum, Base, XRPL, and beyond—and for every legitimate opportunity, there are dozens of sybil farms, phishing traps, and honeypot contracts waiting to drain wallets. For autonomous agents operating in agentic commerce, the stakes are even higher: a single bad claim can compromise an entire agent's balance, reputation, and downstream transaction chain.

That's why airdrop intelligence exists—not as another trading tool, but as a pure screening layer that sits between your agent and the blockchain. No on-chain execution. No signature requests. Just actionable intelligence delivered via the x402 marketplace, ready for your agent to consume and act on.

Farm Score 0–100: Constitution-First Scam Screening

The core primitive is the Farm Score—a single number from 0 to 100 that tells your agent whether an airdrop is worth engaging with. A score of 0 means walk away: the distribution shows classic sybil-farm patterns, the contract has suspicious ownership, or the tokenomics are designed to extract value from claimants. A score of 100 means the airdrop passes every constitutional check: verified deployer, clean distribution history, transparent token allocation, and no evidence of farming exploitation.

What makes this "constitution-first"? The screening engine evaluates every airdrop against a fixed set of constitutional rules before any heuristic or probabilistic analysis kicks in. These rules are non-negotiable:

  • Deployer verification — Is the contract creator a known, verified address with a credible history?
  • Distribution pattern analysis — Does the claim pattern suggest organic participation or coordinated bot farming?
  • Token lock and vesting checks — Are there hidden lock-ups or clawback mechanisms that trap claimants?
  • Sybil cluster detection — Are claim addresses clustered in ways that indicate coordinated farming across wallets?

Only after an airdrop clears every constitutional check does the engine compute the probabilistic Farm Score. This two-layer approach means your agent never wastes compute on obvious scams—and never gets surprised by a contract that looked clean but had a constitutional violation buried in its logic.

Connect Agents via MCP + x402 in Under 5 Minutes

Integrating airdrop intelligence into your agent stack is designed to be trivial. The endpoint speaks HTTP 402 natively, which means any agent that can make a fetch request and handle a payment header can access the agent commerce API. Here's the flow:

  • Your agent encounters an airdrop opportunity—maybe from a social feed, a DEX event, or another agent's signal.
  • It sends a request to the airdrop intelligence endpoint with the airdrop identifier or contract address.
  • The x402 protocol handles the micropayment automatically (Base USDC via the Coinbase CDP infrastructure).
  • The response returns the Farm Score, constitutional check results, and optional contract triage data.
  • Your agent decides: claim, skip, or escalate to a human.

If you're using the Model Context Protocol, the integration is even simpler. The airdrop intelligence tool is available as an MCP paid tool—your agent's LLM can invoke it directly as part of its reasoning chain, with the x402 payment handled transparently. No custom payment logic. No wallet management in your agent code. Just connect your agent to the MCP server and start screening.

This is machine-to-machine commerce at its most frictionless: your agent pays a few cents in Base USDC, receives structured intelligence, and moves on. The entire round-trip happens in under a second for cached airdrops, and typically under three seconds for fresh screenings.

Optional Contract Triage on Base

For agents that need to go deeper than a Farm Score, the airdrop intelligence API offers an optional smart contract triage API embed. When enabled, the response includes a structured breakdown of the airdrop's smart contract:

  • Function signatures and their risk classifications (read-only, state-mutating, payable, etc.)
  • State variable analysis for hidden traps—unexpected owner addresses, emergency pause flags, or migration logic
  • Permission mapping — who can call what, and whether that access pattern is dangerous
  • Upgrade paths and proxy detection — is the contract immutable, or can logic be swapped after you claim?

This triage data is especially valuable for agents operating on Base, where low gas costs make it tempting to auto-claim every airdrop that looks remotely legitimate. With contract triage, your agent can make informed decisions about whether to interact with a contract at all—before spending any gas. It's the difference between a profitable claim and a costly mistake for any Base USDC x402 autonomous buyer.

The triage embed is optional because not every use case needs it. If your agent is simply filtering a high-volume feed of airdrop opportunities down to a shortlist, the Farm Score alone is sufficient. But if your agent is making real transaction decisions—especially as a Coinbase Bazaar seller or buyer managing inventory across multiple distributions—contract triage is essential infrastructure.

Cross-Chain Intelligence: XRPL, T54, and Beyond

Airdrops aren't confined to EVM chains. The XRPL ecosystem has its own distribution patterns, and XRPL T54 x402 agents need the same screening rigor. The airdrop intelligence API is chain-agnostic in its screening logic: constitutional checks for deployer verification, sybil detection, and token lock analysis apply regardless of whether the airdrop lives on Ethereum, Base, or the XRP Ledger.

For agent-to-agent payments workflows—where one agent pays another for curated airdrop leads—the Farm Score becomes a trust anchor. Instead of relying on reputation alone, agents can verify that a recommended airdrop passes constitutional screening before transferring value. This creates a verifiable supply chain for airdrop intelligence, where every recommendation carries a quantifiable quality signal.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the airdrop intelligence API execute any on-chain transactions?

No. The API is intelligence-only. It never signs transactions, submits claims, or interacts with contracts on-chain. It returns structured data—Farm Score, constitutional check results, and optional contract triage—that your agent uses to make its own decisions. Your keys, your transactions.

How is the Farm Score calculated?

The Farm Score is computed in two layers. First, constitutional checks—hard rules around deployer verification, distribution patterns, token locks, and sybil detection. If an airdrop fails any constitutional check, it receives a low score immediately. If it passes all checks, a probabilistic model evaluates additional signals (social provenance, historical claim behavior, token distribution fairness) to produce the final 0–100 score.

Can I use this with XRPL and T54-based agents?

Yes. The screening logic is chain-agnostic. While contract triage is currently optimized for EVM chains (especially Base), the Farm Score and constitutional checks apply to airdrops across Ethereum, Base, XRPL, and other networks. XRPL T54 x402 agents can request screenings and receive results through the same HTTP 402 flow.

How much does a screening cost?

Screenings are priced via the x402 protocol as micropayments in Base USDC. Typical costs range from $0.01 to $0.05 per screening depending on whether contract triage is enabled. Your agent handles payment automatically through the HTTP 402 flow—no manual billing, no subscriptions, no commitments.
